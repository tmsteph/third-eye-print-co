# Third Eye Print Co. Service Expansion Roadmap

Updated: 2026-03-06

## Goal

Expand the current apparel-focused site into a quote-first print services site that can sell a broader catalog similar to 4over without pretending the business already has a full self-serve ecommerce stack.

## 4over Benchmark Categories

Reference categories reviewed on 2026-03-06 from current 4over pages:

- Marketing print: business cards, flyers, postcards, brochures, booklets, folders, door hangers, menus, rack cards.
- Signs and banners: banners, rigid signs, posters, event displays, adhesive graphics.
- Labels and stickers: roll labels, stickers, product labeling.
- Packaging: boxes, hang tags, inserts, lightweight branded packaging pieces.
- Promo and direct mail: promo products, campaigns, mailing support.

## What Was Implemented Today

- Broadened the public-facing catalog on the homepage beyond screen printing and embroidery.
- Added quote form fields for service type, art status, deadline, budget, and fulfillment.
- Added quote presets for common bundled jobs so visitors can request mixed-service orders quickly.
- Disabled deposit checkout automatically when Stripe is not configured, instead of letting the button fail.

## Phase 1: Quote-First Expansion

Timeline: immediate

- Offer quote intake for business cards, flyers, postcards, brochures, stickers, labels, banners, signs, and packaging support.
- Use trusted trade partners for products that are not produced in-house.
- Keep pricing quote-based for non-apparel categories until there is enough margin data to publish ranges confidently.
- Route every lead into one shared pipeline with a clear service type.

## Phase 2: Operational Packaging

Timeline: next 1-3 weeks

- Build a supplier matrix by category: product, minimums, turnaround, blind-ship support, proofing flow, margin target.
- Define what is in-house vs sourced vs do-not-sell yet.
- Create reusable quote templates for apparel, paper print, signage, labels, and mixed bundles.
- Add internal art checklist: print size, bleed, color mode, stock, finish, packaging notes.

## Phase 3: Conversion Layer

Timeline: after Stripe goes live

- Add approved-order deposits tied to quote IDs instead of generic deposits.
- Add service-specific checkout links for standard jobs with stable pricing.
- Add proof approval and payment status to lead records.
- Send payment links automatically after quote approval.

## Phase 4: Catalog Depth

Timeline: after quoting operations are stable

- Launch dedicated landing pages for:
  - business cards and handouts
  - stickers and labels
  - banners and signs
  - brand launch kits
  - event kits
  - retail packaging support
- Add gallery assets and customer examples for each category.
- Publish pricing ranges and minimums where margins are predictable.

## Phase 5: Wholesale and Repeat Orders

Timeline: later

- Add repeat-order flows for teams, events, and brands.
- Add stored specs for returning clients.
- Add blind-ship and reseller workflows if trade fulfillment becomes material.
- Add direct mail and promo sourcing only after the supplier process is reliable.

## Information Still Needed

- Which product categories you are willing to sell immediately, even if sourced through partners.
- Which categories must stay in-house only.
- Minimum order quantities you want shown publicly for paper print, stickers, signs, and packaging.
- Whether you want to position the site as consumer-facing, B2B, or both.
- Brand assets: real gallery photos, preferred turnaround promises, and any must-sell categories.
